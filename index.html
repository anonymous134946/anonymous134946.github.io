RAF: Relativistic Adversarial Feedback For Universal Speech Synthesis

Anonymous Submission

Abstract

We propose Relativistic Adversarial Feedback (RAF), a novel training objective for Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs)-based vocoders that improves fidelity on the source dataset and generalization to unseen scenarios. While GAN-based vocoders benefit from advanced generator and discriminator architectures, their training objectives do not sufficiently encourage generators to learn generalizable representations and often lead to limited capturing of the training data distribution. RAF addresses this problem by leveraging speech self-supervised learning (SSL) models to assist discriminators in sample quality evaluation, encouraging the generator to learn more generalizable representations. Furthermore, we utilize relativistic pairing for real and fake waveforms to enable comprehensive modeling of the training data distribution. Experiments on five datasets demonstrate that RAF consistently improves fidelity and generalization across three GAN-based vocoders, validated through both objective and subjective evaluations. Comparative studies further confirm the effectiveness of our method as an adversarial training framework for neural vocoders.

Relativistic Adversarial Feedback (RAF): Concept and Formulation

Relativistic Adversarial Feedback (RAF) is a novel training framework for GAN-based neural vocoders that improves fidelity and generalization by leveraging pretrained self-supervised learning (SSL) speech models to guide the discriminator. RAF introduces relativistic pairing of real and fake waveform samples to enable better modeling of the data distribution.

Key Components

  • Quality Gap (Q): Measures perceptual discrepancies between real and generated waveforms using SSL embeddings and multi-resolution STFT distance.
  • Discriminator Gap (d): Represents the relativistic difference in discriminator outputs for paired real and fake samples.

Mathematical Formulation

Given ground truth waveform \( y \) and generated waveform \( G(x) \) conditioned on input \( x \), the quality gap is computed as:

\[ Q(y, G(x)) = \left[ \alpha_W Q_W, \alpha_H Q_H, \alpha_M Q_M \right] \in \mathbb{R}^{B \times 3} \]

where each component corresponds to SSL-based metrics (WavLM, HuBERT) and M-STFT distance, scaled by constants \(\alpha_W, \alpha_H, \alpha_M\).

The discriminator gap is defined as the softplus-activated difference of discriminator outputs:

\[ d(y, G(x)) = \mathrm{softplus}\big(D(y) - D(G(x))\big) \in \mathbb{R}^{B \times 3} \]

The adversarial training objective minimizes the error between the discriminator gap and quality gap:

\[ \mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{adv}}(D; G) = \mathbb{E}_{y,x} \Big\| d(y, G(x)) - Q(y, G(x)) \Big\|_2^2 \]

The generator seeks to reduce the discriminator gap:

\[ \mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{adv}}(G; D) = \mathbb{E}_{y,x} \big[d(y, G(x)) \big] \]

Through these mechanisms, RAF encourages generators to capture the full diversity of the training data distribution, leading to enhanced synthesis quality and robustness in unseen scenarios.
